# Privacy

This project ingests and republishes public scientific data about near-Earth
objects, their close approaches to Earth, and the announcements that
announced them. It does not collect, process, or store any personal
information about visitors.

## What we publish

- Near-Earth object records and close-approach predictions sourced from NASA
  JPL's Small-Body Database, the CNEOS close-approach feed, NASA Sentry,
  and ESA NEOCC (no personal data)
- Discovery attributions naming the survey program that first reported each
  object (program-level, e.g. "Catalina Sky Survey," not individuals)
- Bibliographic metadata for scientific publications (titles, authors as
  listed on the publications themselves, DOIs, journal names) — public
  scholarly metadata sourced from the NASA Astrophysics Data System,
  Crossref, and the IAU Minor Planet Center

## What we collect from visitors

Nothing. The public site has no analytics, no cookies, and no user accounts.

## Server logs

The hosting providers (Vercel, Neon, Cloudflare R2) maintain their own
operational logs per their respective privacy policies. We do not aggregate
or analyze visitor logs.
